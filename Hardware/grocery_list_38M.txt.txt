## 3. Technical Justification

### [cite_start]The Core: Quantinuum H2-1 [cite: 4, 5]
* **Role:** Generates the Topologically Protected State.
* **Why this model?** The simulation requires a **Chern Number ≥ 4** to penetrate the vacuum noise floor. Superconducting qubits (IBM/Google) currently struggle with the coherence times required for this specific topology. The H2-1's trapped-ion architecture allows for "all-to-all" connectivity, enabling the complex braiding required to create the protection layer.

### [cite_start]The Receiver: 16-Channel SNSPD [cite: 12, 13]
* **Role:** Detects the "Coherence Shift" (Signal Arrival).
* **Why this model?** Our Python simulation (`Detector` class) assumes a jitter of **50ps** and efficiency of **95%**. Standard APDs (Avalanche Photodiodes) have 300ps jitter and 60% efficiency, which would bury the signal in noise. The SNSPD is non-negotiable for proving FTL arrival.

### [cite_start]The Coupler: Sapphire Whispering-Gallery Resonator [cite: 8, 9]
* **Role:** The "Antenna" that interfaces the Qubits with the Vacuum.
* **Why this model?** We need a High-Q cavity to store the PHz resonance without dissipation. Sapphire at cryogenic temperatures offers a Q-factor > 10^9, essentially trapping the "ringing" of the vacuum long enough for the data to modulate it.
