## II. The Cosmic Cycle

### Phase 1: Coherence Minimum (The Big Bang)

**Cycle Position: 0.00**  
**Duration: ~10⁻⁴³ seconds**

At the coherence minimum:
- No classical spacetime exists

- What we call the "Big Bang singularity"

In standard cosmology, this is a true singularity where physics breaks down. In our framework, it's simply the **trough of the coherence wave**—a regular, repeating feature of cosmic dynamics.

The "singularity" isn't where physics ends. It's where the coherence field is at its minimum configuration, preparing to rise again.

### Phase 2: Coherence Rising (Inflation)

**Cycle Position: 0.00 - 0.15**  
**Duration: ~13.8 billion years (to present)**

As coherence rises from its minimum:

**Cycle Position: ~0.15 - 0.25**  
**Duration: ~10¹¹ years**

Near the balance point (C ≈ S):
- Spacetime appears maximally flat (Ω ≈ 1)
- Conditions favor complex structure: galaxies, planets, life
- Quantum and classical realms are most clearly separated
- Observers like us can emerge and contemplate the cosmos

**This is not coincidence.** The "flatness problem" in cosmology—why is space so perfectly flat?—resolves naturally. We observe flat space because we exist near the balance point. Observers couldn't exist in the highly curved phases.

### Phase 4: Entropy Dominant (Expansion Era)

**Cycle Position: 0.25 - 0.75**  
**Duration: ~10¹⁴ years**

As entropy begins to dominate:
- Dark energy drives accelerating expansion
- Stars exhaust their fuel and die

This is the phase standard cosmology predicts as our future—an ever-emptier, ever-colder universe.

### Phase 5: Entropy Maximum (Heat Death)

**Cycle Position: 0.75 - 0.90**  
**Duration: ~10¹⁰⁰ years**

At the entropy maximum:

- Only low-energy photons remain

Standard cosmology stops here—eternal heat death. But in our framework, this is just another phase.

### Phase 6: Coherence Resurgence (Quantum Rebirth)

**Cycle Position: 0.90 - 1.00**  
**Duration: ~10¹⁰⁰+ years**

As the coherence wave begins its return:

- New correlations begin forming

And then the cycle repeats. Not a repetition of the same events, but a new verse in the eternal song.

## III. Why Space Appears Flat

### The Flatness Problem

One of the great puzzles of cosmology: why is the universe so flat?

The density parameter Ω measures the ratio of actual density to critical density. Observations show Ω = 1.000 ± 0.02—essentially perfect flatness. But this requires incredible fine-tuning in standard cosmology. Even tiny deviations from Ω = 1 in the early universe would have grown enormously by now.

### The Wave Solution

In our framework, flatness isn't fine-tuned—it's **selected by observation**.

Spacetime curvature emerges from the imbalance between coherence and entropy:

**Curvature ∝ |C - S|**

At the balance point (C = S), curvature approaches zero. Space appears flat.

We observe flat space because:
1. We exist near the balance point in the cosmic cycle
2. Complex observers can only emerge near balance points
3. Far from balance, curvature is too extreme for stable structures

This is a weak anthropic argument, but with a crucial difference: we've **specified the mechanism**. The flatness isn't mysterious—it's a consequence of wave dynamics.

## IV. The Arrow of Time

### Why Does Time Flow Forward?

In fundamental physics, most equations are time-reversible. So why do we experience time flowing in one direction?

Standard answer: entropy increase (the second law of thermodynamics).

But this just pushes the question back: why does entropy increase?

### Surfing the Wave

In our framework, the arrow of time is the **direction of wave propagation**.

We don't experience the full wave—we experience our local slice of it. As the wave propagates, we perceive:
- The past: regions the wave has already traversed
- The present: our local wavefront
- The future: regions the wave hasn't reached yet

Time's arrow isn't fundamental—it's **perspectival**. We're embedded in the wave, moving with it, unable to see backward along its path.

This explains:
- Why time seems to flow uniformly (we're riding a smooth wave)
- Why entropy increases (wave propagation direction)
- Why we can't reverse time (we'd have to reverse the wave)
- Why the past seems fixed (wave has already passed through)

The "block universe" of relativity and the "flowing time" of experience are both correct—from different vantage points relative to the wave.
